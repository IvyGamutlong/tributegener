Soichiro Honda

Founder of Honda Motor Company

A maverick in a country not known for its willingness to accept nonconformists, Soichiro Honda (1906-1991) created an automobile giant despite the opposition of the Japanese government. One of his company cars, the Accord, was a best-selling model in the American market.

Here's a time line of Soichiro Honda:

  • 1906 - Born in in rural Iwata-gun, Japan
  • 1922 - He graduated from the Futamata Senior Elementary School and began his career as an apprentice auto repairman for Arto Shokai, after which he established a branch shop for the firm in Hamamatsu.
  • 1928 - He organized the Tohai Seiki Company to manufacture piston rings, some of which were sold to Toyota
  • 1930's - It seemed his would become one of the hundreds of small shops that supplied the major companies in what still was a small domestic market.
  • 1936 -Honda raced a turbocharged Ford in the "1st Japan Automobile Race" at Tamagawa Speedway.
  • 1946 - Proceeeds to found the Honda Technical Institute.
  • 1948 - He started producing a complete motorized bicycle, the type A.
  • 1949 - Honda designed and produced Type D, a motorcycle with a pressed-steel frame and 2 stroke.
  • 1959 - Honda Motorcycles opened its first dealership in United States.
  • 1973 - He retired as the President of ihs company.
  • 1980 - People's magazine placed him on their "25 most intruing people of te year" dubbing him "the japanese Henry Ford".
  • 1982 - ASME established the Soichiro Honda Medal in recognition of Mr. Honda's achievements.
  • 1983 - He stayed on as a director and was appointed "Supreme Advisor".
  • 1989 - He was inducted into the Automotive Hall of Fame near Detroit.
  • 1991 - Soichiro Honda died days before the Hungarian Grand Prix, of liver failure.
